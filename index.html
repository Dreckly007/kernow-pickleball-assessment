What is this form & how do I use it?

This assessment tool can be used in two ways:

  • By players to assess their own pickleball skill level and identify areas for improvement.
  • By coaches, instructors, or club assessors to evaluate a player's current ability and development needs.

The tool measures performance across key areas of the game and provides a structured, objective assessment of overall skill level.

Each skill is scored from 1 to 10. The scores are then converted into a 1 to 5 rating scale, weighted according to importance, and used to estimate a DUPR-style rating with decimal precision.

The assessment highlights strengths, identifies areas for improvement, and provides coaching recommendations based on lower-scoring skills.

How to use it

  • Complete the assessment based on performance during matches, practice games, drill sessions, club assessments, or your recent playing experience.
  • If assessing yourself, score based on what you do consistently in real game situations, not what you are capable of doing occasionally.
  • If assessing another player, score based on what you observe consistently over the course of the session.
  • Each skill box describes the behaviours and abilities that should be demonstrated regularly.
  • Use the ? icon for a more detailed explanation of what separates lower, middle, and higher scores.
  • Score each skill from 1 to 10 based on consistent performance, not one-off great shots or isolated mistakes.
  • Results are displayed on a converted 1 to 5 scale, allowing for decimal ratings such as 3.7 or 2.8.

Important note

This is not an official DUPR rating. It is a coaching and assessment estimate based on observed performance.
